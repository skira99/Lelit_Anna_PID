The PID controller I had chose initially is the Inkbird ITC-106, however I quickly found that there were limitations to the auto-tuning, and despite my best efforts to tune it manually following more rigorous methods (Ziegler–Nichols method), I eventually decided to try the more commercially oriented ITC-100. I also took the opportunity to redo the mounting of the PID, as external mounting I had tried was interfering with loading the portafilter into the machine. This required cutting out a square hole in the top of the case, and relocating the SSR a bit, which made it quite a tight fit in the already cramped machine. Despite this, I was able to mount everything quite cleanly and securely, and the new controller has much tighter control over the temperature, with a slightly different calculation algorithm.

Changes:

-Moved SSR behind water tank
